How It Started

From the CIA to York, PA

The Copper Crust Company was founded by Sean and Nicole Austin, graduates of the Culinary Institute of America whose culinary vision was shaped by years working in the high-pressure kitchens of New York City and Washington D.C. — and by formative travels through Portugal, Italy, and France, where artisan baking is a way of life.

Those experiences — watching a baker in Lisbon pull a tray of perfectly bronzed pastéis from a wood-fired oven, eating croque-monsieurs in a Parisian brasserie at 7am, mastering the long ferment of Neapolitan pizza dough — all live inside everything that comes out of the Copper Crust kitchen.

The Name

What "Copper" Means

The "Copper" in our name refers to a very specific moment — the caramelized, burnished hue of a stone-hearth bake when heat, time, and craft converge into something extraordinary.

It's the moment a croissant achieves that deep amber-copper sheen. The instant a pizza crust blisters and chars at the edges. That color is not an accident — it's the result of doing things the right way, without shortcuts.

While we source a few specific traditional rolls — our Kaiser and Sub — to ensure the perfect sandwich bite, our heart belongs to that hearth and everything that comes out of it.
