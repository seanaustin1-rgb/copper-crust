The Laminated Croissant

Three days.
Hundreds of layers.
One moment.

Our croissants are made using a 72-hour lamination process — butter and dough folded together in precise sequences, cold-rested between each turn to build the structure that produces that audible shatter and the honeycomb interior beneath. You cannot rush lamination. The process takes three days. The result takes about three minutes to disappear.

The Bakers

Trained at

Our founding baker trained at the Culinary Institute of America — one of the most rigorous culinary programs in the country. That education lives in every score we make on a sourdough crust and every layer we fold into a croissant.

We have been operating in York since 2012. More than a decade of daily bakes means we know this community's palate, its rhythms, and what it deserves: honest, exceptional bread and pastry at a fair price.
